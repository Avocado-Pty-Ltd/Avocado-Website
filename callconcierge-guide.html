Getting Started

1. Account Creation

Download the app and sign in. You can use your Google account or sign in with your phone number.

2. Onboarding Flow

After logging in, you will see the "Setup Your Business" screen. Follow these 3 steps to activate your service:

1

Choose Your Phone Number

Search for a local mobile number for your business. Tap Search Available Numbers, select a number from the list, and tap Continue.

2

Configure Your Virtual Assistant

Tell your virtual assistant about your business so it can answer customer questions:

  • Business Name: Enter your company name (e.g., "Joe's Pizza")
  • Business Context: Describe your services, hours, location, and pricing. The more details you provide, the smarter your agent will be.
  • Timezone: Select your local timezone
3

Verify Your Personal Phone

We need your personal mobile number to transfer calls to you when a customer asks to speak to a human.

  1. Enter your Personal Mobile Number
  2. Tap Send Verification Code
  3. Enter the 6-digit code you receive via SMS
  4. Tap Verify Code
  5. Once verified, tap Activate Service to finish setup

The more detailed your Business Context, the better your virtual assistant can answer customer questions. Include hours, pricing, FAQs, and any special services!

Using the Phone

Making Calls

Go to the Phone tab to make outbound calls.

  • Keypad: Dial any number manually
  • Caller ID: Calls will appear to come from your Business Number, keeping your personal number private

Receiving Calls

When a customer calls your business number, here's what happens:

🤖 Your Virtual Assistant Answers

Your virtual assistant picks up immediately and answers questions using the "Business Context" you provided.

📞 Call Transfer

If the customer asks to speak to you, your virtual assistant will put them on hold and call your personal phone.

Accepting the Transfer

You have two options to accept an incoming transfer:

Notification

When the notification appears on your phone, tap the Transfer to me button. This will instantly trigger the handoff.

Phone Call

Answer your personal phone. You will hear: "Press 1 to accept this customer call." Press 1 to connect.

Once you accept, your virtual assistant will drop off and you'll be speaking directly to the customer—seamless handoff!

Virtual Assistant Training

Your virtual receptionist's intelligence comes from the Business Context you provide. The more detailed and accurate this information, the better your agent will handle calls and answer customer questions.

What is Business Context?

Business Context is the knowledge base that powers your virtual receptionist. It includes everything your agent needs to know to represent your business professionally—services, hours, pricing, location, policies, and frequently asked questions.

How Your Virtual Assistant Uses This Information

When a customer calls and asks "What are your hours?" or "How much does a haircut cost?", your virtual assistant searches its Business Context to provide accurate, natural-sounding answers. Think of it as training a new employee—give them all the information they need to succeed!

Best Practices

  • Include specific business hours with days and times
  • List all services with pricing ranges
  • Add your location and service areas
  • Include common FAQs customers ask
  • Mention booking or appointment processes
  • Add policies (cancellation, deposits, refunds)
  • Include any special offers or discounts
  • Keep information up to date

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Being too vague (e.g., "We do plumbing stuff")
  • Forgetting to update when hours or prices change
  • Not including emergency or after-hours information
  • Missing important policies that affect customers
  • Using industry jargon customers won't understand
  • Leaving out your location or service areas
  • Not mentioning accepted payment methods

Review and update your Business Context every few months, or whenever something changes. Outdated information leads to frustrated customers and missed opportunities!

Transfer Notification Actions

When a customer asks to speak to you, you'll receive a notification with a "Transfer to me" button. Tap it to instantly take over the call—your virtual assistant will hand off seamlessly.

What You'll See

The notification shows:

  • Caller's phone number (or name if in your contacts)
  • Brief summary of what they're calling about
  • Quick action button to accept the transfer

Managing Notification Settings

To ensure you receive notifications:

  1. Open your device's Settings app
  2. Go to Notifications (or Apps > CallConcierge)
  3. Make sure notifications are enabled
  4. Enable sounds and banners for important alerts

Keep notifications enabled even when you're busy—you can always decline transfers, but you'll still see a summary of what your virtual assistant handled!

Troubleshooting

Having issues? Here are solutions to common problems.

Calls Not Coming Through

Symptoms

You're not receiving notifications when customers call, or calls aren't being answered by your virtual assistant.

Solutions

  • Check that notification permissions are enabled for CallConcierge
  • Ensure the app is allowed to run in the background
  • Verify your subscription is active in Settings
  • Check you have a stable internet connection

Virtual Assistant Not Answering Correctly

Symptoms

The virtual assistant gives wrong information or says "I don't know" to questions.

Solutions

  • Update your Business Context with more detailed information
  • Include specific answers to common customer questions
  • Make sure pricing, hours, and services are current
  • Review call transcripts to see what customers are asking

Transfer Not Working

Symptoms

You tap "Transfer to me" but don't receive the call, or the customer gets disconnected.

Solutions

  • Verify your personal phone number is correct in Settings
  • Check you have mobile signal on your personal phone
  • Make sure you're not on Do Not Disturb mode
  • Try the phone call option (press 1) if the notification doesn't work

Can't Hear Customer / Audio Issues

Symptoms

You can't hear the customer after transfer, or they can't hear you.

Solutions

  • Check microphone permissions for the app
  • Ensure your phone's volume is up
  • Try removing and reinserting headphones/earbuds
  • Restart the app and try again

App Crashes or Running Slow

Symptoms

CallConcierge freezes, crashes, or takes a long time to load.

Solutions

  • Update to the latest version from the app store
  • Restart your device
  • Check available storage space on your device
  • Try uninstalling and reinstalling the app (your data is saved)
